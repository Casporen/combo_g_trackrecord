Methodology

  • Pre-registration. Hypothesis, expected sign and thresholds are frozen before any out-of-sample data is touched. The signal version on the live book is locked.
  • Anti-leakage IS/OOS split. A hard date cut separates the discovery period from the frozen out-of-sample window. The OOS window is evaluated once.
  • Significance. Permutation and bootstrap, plus a heteroskedasticity-robust regression that decomposes the raw return into market-beta and residual alpha — so the edge is the part that is not just market exposure.
  • Costs. Fees, funding and a conservative slippage are charged to every trade.
  • What is not shown. No feature, threshold, or signal parameter is published. The track record proves results, not the recipe.

Graveyard — tested & discarded

A track record is only credible next to the ideas that failed. Mechanism stated, recipe never.

  • Funding carry. The "free yield" is compensation for tail risk — no risk-adjusted edge after costs.
  • Dump continuation. Real but validated-insufficient: signal too thin to deploy after controls.
  • Short-squeeze (short crowding → squeeze). Tail is real but the predictive part is momentum + beta; crowding adds no robust edge.
  • Liquidation absorption. The dislocation exists but is un-tradeable — you catch a falling knife.
  • HLP forced inventory. Significant on huge N but wrong-signed, non-directional, sub-cost — not exploitable.
  • Low-beta / listing drift. No forced actor that survives beta and momentum controls.
